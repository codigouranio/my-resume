## Private Life Journal (AI-Context Feed)
This feature provides a clean, social-media-style interface for users to capture and organize their personal life in a private, chronological feed.
### Core Experience

- Posts are displayed vertically in a familiar social-network layout.
- Each post supports up to 500 words of text, plus multiple photos and documents (.pdf, .txt, .md, .docx, images, etc.).
- Users can backdate posts to accurately reflect past events, achievements, or memories.

### Content Types
A post can represent anything the user wants to remember: an event, achievement, milestone, lesson learned, problem solved, travel memory, reflection, or simple daily note.
#### Social-Style Interactions (kept private)

- Like, ❤️ heart, ✏️ pencil (edit note), 🏅 medal, 🏆 award, and other custom reactions/emojis.
- Users can reply to their own posts (threaded self-reflection).

#### Search & Organization

- Powerful full-text search across all posts and attached documents.
- Posts are fully editable and deletable at any time.

#### AI Integration (Core Value)

Every post is enabled by default to serve as context for the AI.
A simple toggle switch lets the user disable any post so it is excluded from AI context.
Posts can be tagged to one or more specific resumes or profiles (e.g., “Software Engineer Resume”, “Freelance Portfolio”, “Personal Growth”).
All enabled posts automatically become rich, living context for the AI when generating or improving resumes, cover letters, interview prep, or career coaching.

## Dashboard Placement
This entire journal lives in the user’s personal dashboard under the AI Context tab, giving a single place to manage everything the AI “knows” about them.
